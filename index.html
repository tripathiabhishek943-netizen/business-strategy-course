Case Study — Southwest Airlines
Meet Southwest Airlines ✈️
🎓
Abhishek Tripathi
Now we apply everything we've learned to one of the most studied business strategies in history. Southwest Airlines has been profitable for 47 consecutive years in an industry where most airlines routinely lose money. How? Let's find out.
Founded
1967 — Dallas, Texas
Strategy Type
Cost Leadership — Focused
Consecutive Profitable Years
47 Years (1973–2019)
Annual Passengers
~130 million per year
The Paradox: Airlines are historically the worst industry for investor returns. Yet Southwest has returned consistent profits for nearly 5 decades. The answer is pure strategy.
🎙 Southwest Airlines has achieved 47 consecutive years of profitability in one of the world's toughest industries. Understanding how they did it will make all our strategic concepts concrete and real.
